<p><p>Lentiviral vectors are essential in gene therapy, providing a stable delivery mechanism for genetic material into dividing and non-dividing cells. The market includes several types of viral vectors:</p><p>1. **Retrovirus (RV)**: Primarily targets dividing cells, making it suitable for certain cancer therapies but limited in applications.</p><p>  </p><p>2. **Adenovirus (AdV)**: Effective in delivering large genetic payloads, it can infect both dividing and non-dividing cells, though it elicits strong immune responses.</p><p>3. **Adeno-associated Virus (AAV)**: Known for its low immunogenicity and ability to integrate into host DNA, AAV is favored for long-term expression in gene therapy applications.</p></p>
