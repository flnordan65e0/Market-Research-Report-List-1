<p><p>Natural refrigerants are substances that occur naturally in the environment and are used in cooling systems, such as air conditioning, refrigeration, and heat pumps. Unlike synthetic refrigerants, they have minimal environmental impact, with low global warming potential (GWP) and zero ozone depletion potential (ODP). Common examples include ammonia (NH3), carbon dioxide (CO2), hydrocarbons (like propane and isobutane), and water.</p><p>The Natural Refrigerant Market is experiencing significant growth due to increasing environmental regulations and the global shift toward sustainable cooling solutions. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.5% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the phase-out of synthetic refrigerants like hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) under international agreements such as the Kigali Amendment to the Montreal Protocol. Industries such as food retail, industrial refrigeration, and HVAC systems are increasingly adopting natural refrigerants to meet regulatory requirements and reduce carbon footprints.</p><p>Latest trends in the market include the development of energy-efficient systems, advancements in CO2-based transcritical refrigeration, and the rising use of hydrocarbons in domestic and commercial applications. Additionally, the growing demand for eco-friendly solutions in emerging economies is expected to further propel market expansion.</p></p>
