<p><ul><li>Collagen</li><li>Oxidized Regenerated Cellulose</li><li>Gelatin</li><li>Polysaccharide</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The anti-bleeding drugs market is primarily categorized into four types: Collagen, Oxidized Regenerated Cellulose (ORC), Gelatin, and Polysaccharides. Collagen is utilized for its natural hemostatic properties, effectively promoting clot formation. ORC acts as a scaffolding for clot development and is favored for its biocompatibility. Gelatin, being absorbable and easy to use, aids in rapid hemostasis. Polysaccharides enhance coagulation through absorption of blood and promoting cellular response, making them essential in surgical and trauma settings.</p></p>
